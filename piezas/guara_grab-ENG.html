GUARAPICHE BLUES
(Emilio Mendoza)
C/Am/G   72  ternary div.  

This piece is intended to denounce the oil spill which occurred on February 4, 2012, in the Guarapiche River, Monagas State, that supplies water to its capital Maturín. It is an accident that repeatedly keeps happening until today. It was one of the biggest spills ever on a river. The blues and the joroponovo are closely related as siblings in their rhythmical structures, either simultaneously or alternately. This blues appears in major and minor modes, also modulating its key for the last verse and is the only track in the CD with texts in the voice of María Rivas, as a special tribute to her. It is at the same time a joroponovo which narrates in the style of a “mini-opera,” the regretful memories of a local girl who discovers her river one early morning completely suffocated with oil. Then the disaster is announced by the media in the reading of the news by an anchor with indifference and detachment, and finally those affected shout as a group in protest for not knowing the truth of what happened to their river.

Video
 Due to lack of financing, the video could only be produced in December 2019, with production culminating in January 2020, through the School of Cinema and Television of Caracas, ESCINETV, with the co-production of María Teresa Capriles and Ozono Jazz, photography by Harold Cooper and direction by Jorge Gómez Plazola. A group of more than twenty students worked on the video clip as part of a course organized by the school, making a pre-premiere with eminent Venezuelan cinema personalities such as Leopoldo Izaguirre, Román Chalbaud and the composer Beatriz Bilbao. See the 2020 activities about this event. When the video was released on July 25, 2020, two terrible spills occurred in Venezuela, in Falcón and Anzoátegui states, without any reaction from the Government despite staining the beaches of the Chichiirivichi, Morrocoy and Golfo Triste keys. There was another spill on the island of Mauritius, in Africa.

 

Ozono's ecological activism has focused on two aspects of the diverse range of problems that humanity has: noise and accidents in the oil industry, which correspond to us as Venezuelans in a country that has been a large producer of oil for more than 100 years. In the case of what the author calls the "Black Tragedies", already in 1984 he made with his music a very experimental video for the times, on the Tacoa tragedy, please see the video-art page on this topic and the spill.

The recording of Guarapiche was stopped for a long time since it was desired that María Rivas would sing in it because the entire album was a tribute to her for all her background in ecological activism through music. She was the first singer in Venezuela to awaken ecological awareness in her concerts, designated "Godmother of Inparques" in 1992 and received the Phelps Prize for Ecology in 1993.

In September 2018, she was in Venezuela in promotion and hustle for her Grammy award nomination, which she attended in the USA. On her return, she fell ill with bronchopneumonia as a result of a lung metastasis and she remained that way until a day before her departure to Spain when María was able to record this piece at her home in El Toronjil in a single take despite her condition, in the studio of Miguel Chacón, her previous husband.

This was apparently her last recording before her death in 2019. The author had no idea of ​​María's connection to the Maturín area and it was an amazing coincidence that she sang the blues with the theme of the Guarapiche river when her father was from that area, a fact which produced an intense emotional delivery to the music, without having rehearsed it before.
