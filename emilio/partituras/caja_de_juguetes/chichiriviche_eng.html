La Caja de Juguetes
Chichiriviche
Concierto-Instalación
para un ejecutante barrigón
1975 / 5'

No ha sido estrenada

Intrument:
        The following object is necessary to make the instrument:
  • a car's ventilador belt
  • some plastic fishing thread
  • a few empty beer and coke cans, old, rusty, bent, broken or complete.
Tie the cans with the thread in various rows hanging from the ventilator belt, similar to a glass chime.

Equipment:

       A radio, cd player or recorded sound-producing device, with salsa music or reggaeton music.


Interpreter:

        A person of about 30-40 years old, with a pot belly. The interpreter is dressed only with a bading suit or shorts, plastic flip-flops, sun glasses, and a small straw hat with a feather, or any small hat. The instrument is carried in the rigth hand, an a liquor/beer bottle half-filled in the left hand. The interpreter has some sand in his legs and in general bears a degenerated semblance.

Action:

        The music of the CD on stage is turned on very loud. Lights go on, the interpreter enters slowly onto the stage from the left side, stops in the middle of it, smiles and produces some sounds with the instrument for 5 seconds. Of course, nothing is heard because the music is too loud. (The beer-can instrument should not be amplified). The person then stops for a moment, looks directly to the audience, takes a drink from the bottle and leaves the stages through the same side that it was entered. Before disappearing from the view of the public, he throws the instrument on the floor, gives it a kick with his foot, and drinks once more from the bottle.
